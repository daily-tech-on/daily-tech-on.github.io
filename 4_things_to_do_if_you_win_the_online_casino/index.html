4 things to do if you win in the online casino

Technology Games

I already admitted it and I will admit it again - occasionally I play the online casino. Not with a real expectation of winning, of course, but just for fun and the “what if” teasing that occurs when some of my numbers appear. I have never won anything more than free spins, but I still dream of traveling to retirement, helping family members and donating my favorite charities.But winning a big jackpot is not necessarily the route to future happiness. Plenty of stories profile winners who soon became losers when they frittered their money away or lifetime friendships were put to the test. Even though we all dream of winning the lottery, not too many of us are prepared to handle what comes next. In fact, your lucky day could turn into your unlucky day if you’re not careful.

With that in mind, let’s take a look at some of the most important things you can do to avoid becoming a loser after winning.

1. Protect That Winning Confirmation and Take Your Time

First, protect your online casino winning confirmation. Make hard copies, store digital copies, invest in a safe, or take the bill to the bank to store it in a safe.

Once your winning confirmation is secure, take the time to contact the casino authorities. This is for several reasons, but mainly to allow the media circle of a big winner to go out and give you time to breathe and plan your new future life. Most lotteries allow winners 180 or 365 days to claim their prize, so taking a week off before claiming yours can do a lot for your mental state.

2. Get professional help

Once you understand how to distribute your earnings, find a lawyer or financial advisor who can help you structure a financial plan for you.

Whatever you do, don't go through the phone book and call the first lawyer or investment advisor you see. Ask your friends and family for someone they recommend and trust.

Another way to find a good advisor is to note the balance between the personal and financial questions he or she asks you. The best advisers will always spend more time figuring out who you are as a person before you structure a plan.

3. Don’t Quit Your Job Just Yet

That's what you've always dreamed of: quitting your job to retire to the islands with a cold tropical drink, right? But don't make this decision too quickly. You have a financial plan to settle and you want to make sure that you have not confused the numbers or the date on your casino winning.

Remember to keep your good fortune at work too. A silent smile is all you need. Otherwise, the word might come out before you're ready and your boss might start looking for someone to replace you.

4. Invest tax efficiently

Once you have determined how much money you will need for your lifestyle and how much you can separate, it is likely that the amount remaining is too large to be placed in a registered savings account. This leaves your wealth in unregistered territory, which makes reducing tax exposure to income-generating investments a priority.

Investments can range from a diverse equity portfolio - which takes your risk tolerance into account - to a permanent life insurance policy, which allows you to grow your money tax-free.

Be prepared to start simple, unless you are already a savvy investor.
